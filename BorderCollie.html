Border Collie

This breed is extremely driven, loyal, and hard-working. Border collies are happiest when they are working or playing, and will only settle down for cuddle time when the workday is done. Therefore, these dogs do best in homes with large, fenced yards, as well as on farms and ranches, rather than apartments.



Lifestyle and Time Commitment:

Border Collies are highly energetic and require regular exercise and mental stimulation. Owners should be prepared to spend a significant amount of time engaging in activities such as walking, running, and playing with their Border Collie.

Space and Living Arrangements:

Border Collies do best in homes with access to a yard or outdoor space where they can run and play. They are not well-suited to apartment living unless they receive ample exercise and mental stimulation.

Costs:

Owning a Border Collie can be costly. In addition to the initial cost of purchasing or adopting a dog, owners should budget for food, grooming, veterinary care, training, and supplies. Border Collies are also prone to certain health issues, so owners should be prepared for potential medical expenses.

Breed and Size:

Border Collies are medium-sized dogs, typically weighing between 30-45 pounds. They are known for their agility and athleticism, making them well-suited for activities such as agility training and herding trials. They have a medium-length double coat that requires regular grooming to prevent matting and tangles.

Commitment and Responsibility:

Border Collies are highly trainable and form strong bonds with their owners. They thrive on mental stimulation and are happiest when they have a job to do. Owners should be prepared to provide plenty of exercise, training, and attention to keep their Border Collie happy and healthy.
